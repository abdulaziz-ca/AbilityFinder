WorkBC Assistive Technology Services

Funding for assistive technology you need to get or keep a job: devices and equipment, ergonomic furniture, communication and hearing devices, ASL interpreting and captioning, workplace modifications — even vehicle modifications for getting to work.

What it is

Assistive Technology Services (ATS) removes equipment barriers between you and employment. It funds assistive devices and technology, ergonomic supports like furniture and lighting, restorative supports, ASL interpreting and captioning, communication and hearing devices for work, workplace access modifications, and vehicle modifications needed for employment. How much is covered depends on your financial circumstances.

Who it is for

Covers work-related needs only — not medical treatments or daily-living items. Delivered under contract to the province with regional offices in Burnaby, Kelowna, Nanaimo and Victoria.

Amount or value

Equipment and supports funded fully or partially, based on your financial circumstances

How to apply

  1. Apply through WorkBC Online Employment Services (apply.workbc.ca) or contact ATS directly.
  2. An ATS specialist works with you to understand your work barrier and equipment needs.
  3. Approved equipment and services are arranged fully or partially funded.

What you may need

  • Proof you are a B.C. resident eligible to work
  • Information about your job, job offer, self-employment or volunteer role
  • Details of the disability-related barrier and equipment needs (ATS helps document this)

Practical tips

  • Commuting barriers count — vehicle modifications and travel-related equipment can be funded when needed for work.
  • You can use ATS while employed to keep your job, not just when job hunting.
  • Email info-ats@workbc.ca if you are unsure whether your situation qualifies.

Timing

Varies by request; contact ATS for current timelines
