Recreation Fee Subsidy Program (RFSP)

Richmond's recreation subsidy for residents in financial hardship — free drop-ins with no cap, and nine tenths off most registered programs.

What it is

Participants get unlimited free admission to many drop-in services and a 90% discount on most registered City and participating community programs, subject to the annual registered-program subsidy cap.

Who it is for

The 90% discount on registered programs is capped at $300 per year in subsidy for children and youth 18 and under, and $100 per year for adults and seniors 19 and over. Free drop-in admission is not capped.

Amount or value

Unlimited free drop-in admission plus 90% off registered programs, up to $300 a year for children and youth or $100 a year for adults and seniors

How to apply

  1. Download the application for the current program year and review the two financial-hardship qualification methods
  2. Complete one application per family or couple and attach the documents for the selected method
  3. Submit by email, mail or at a Richmond recreation facility, Cultural Centre or City Hall
  4. If help is needed, book an in-person support appointment at City Hall
  5. Keep the approval letter and use the subsidy when registering for eligible activities

What you may need

  • Completed current-year application
  • Proof of Richmond residence
  • Government-assistance confirmation or current income and financial-hardship documents required by the form

Practical tips

  • Use the application for the correct program year
  • In-person application help is by appointment, not drop-in
  • Ask before registering whether a program counts toward the annual discounted-program cap

Timing

Current 2026–2027 applications cover September 1, 2026 to August 31, 2027
