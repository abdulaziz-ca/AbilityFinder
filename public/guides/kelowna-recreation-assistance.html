Financial Assistance for Recreation (KFAP)

Kelowna's recreation subsidy for residents on a low income — a credit you spend on a facility pass or on programs.

What it is

Kelowna assesses combined household income from all sources and places an approved credit on the recreation account for passes or City programs.

Who it is for

Apply by emailing the Access to Recreation Department. Assessment can take up to two weeks once all information is received. The income assessment counts income from all sources, including disability benefits and workers' compensation as well as employment, investments, pensions, child and foster child support, rental income and funds held in trust.

Amount or value

A credit toward a Parkinson Recreation Centre pass or City programs

How to apply

  1. Email the Access to Recreation Department to request the current application process
  2. Gather proof of a Kelowna address from the last three months
  3. Get a CRA Income Statement (Option C print, not Notice of Assessment) for each household member who contributes income or expenses
  4. Submit the completed application and all supporting documents
  5. Wait for approval before buying a pass or program; approved credit can then be used during registration

What you may need

  • Recent utility, cable or internet bill, rental agreement, or government document showing a Kelowna address
  • CRA Income Statement for contributing household members
  • Government-assistance confirmation when using that qualifying route
  • Citizenship or permanent-resident information requested by the form

Practical tips

  • Previous purchases are not covered, so wait for approval
  • All income sources count, including disability benefits and income outside Canada
  • A clerk at Parkinson Recreation Centre can check whether processing is complete

Timing

Up to two weeks after all required information is received
