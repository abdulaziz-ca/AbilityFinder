B.C. Access Grant for Students with Disabilities

Non-repayable grant money that replaces B.C. student loans for full-time students with a disability, so you borrow less.

What it is

This non-repayable grant replaces B.C. student loan funding with grant funding for eligible full-time students with a disability at B.C. public post-secondary schools, reducing the amount they borrow.

Who it is for

B.C. public post-secondary schools only. It replaces B.C. student loan funding with grant funding, so it lowers what you have to pay back.

Amount or value

Up to $1,560 per program year ($30 per week of study)

At a glance

Difficulty
Easy
Application

With your student-aid assessment

How to apply

  1. Apply for full-time StudentAid BC funding for the study period
  2. If disability status is not already verified, submit the StudentAid BC Disability Verification Form completed by an accepted medical assessor
  3. Confirm that the school is a B.C. public post-secondary institution
  4. Review the assessment notice in your StudentAid BC account; the grant is assessed with the aid application

What you may need

  • StudentAid BC application
  • Disability Verification Form if status is not already verified
  • Any follow-up financial information StudentAid BC requests

Practical tips

  • There is no separate grant application after disability status is verified
  • Contact the school's accessibility services office early for accommodations and related disability funding
