B.C. Assistance Program for Students with Disabilities

Funding for exceptional education-related services and adaptive equipment — the top-up you turn to once the federal services and equipment grant runs out.

What it is

This grant funds exceptional education-related services and adaptive equipment after all available funding from the Canada Student Grant for Services and Equipment – Students with Disabilities has been used. Apply for the federal grant first.

Who it is for

You can only receive this grant once you have used up all available funds through the Canada Student Grant for Services and Equipment – Students with Disabilities. Apply for that federal grant first. The page does not say whether these amounts are per year or a lifetime limit.

Amount or value

Up to $10,000, or up to $12,000 if an attendant is required at school

At a glance

Difficulty
Medium
Application
Student aid + documented exceptional education costs
Decision timing
After other disability funding is assessed

How to apply

  1. Have StudentAid BC verify your disability status
  2. Meet with the school's accessibility services office to document disability-related services and equipment
  3. Apply first for the Canada Student Grant for Services and Equipment – Students with Disabilities
  4. If eligible costs exceed that grant, have the accessibility office submit the B.C. Assistance Program request and quotes
  5. Wait for written approval before purchasing equipment or committing to services

What you may need

  • Verified disability status
  • Accessibility-services assessment of required services or equipment
  • Supplier quotes or service-cost estimates
  • Evidence that the federal services-and-equipment grant has been used

Practical tips

  • Do not buy equipment before written funding approval
  • The official page does not state whether the maximum is annual or lifetime; ask StudentAid BC before planning around it

Timing

Varies with assessment, quotes and funding review
