Financial Assistance for Recreation (FAR)

Coquitlam gives each eligible family member their own 50 drop-in passes and their own $225 credit toward City admissions, passes and programs.

What it is

Each eligible family member receives 50 drop-in passes and a $225 credit. Income verification can use a CRA statement, government assistance, a red Compass Card or an approved adjudicator or agency signature.

Who it is for

You can verify income four different ways: a CRA Income Statement (the Option C print, not a Notice of Assessment) for each family member who contributes to household income; OR the main applicant's proof of government assistance; OR the main applicant's proof of receiving a red Compass Card; OR a completed adjudicator or agency signature section on the application. You also need proof of Coquitlam residency dated within two months. The red Compass Card route is worth calling out — it is the same card BC transit concession holders already carry.

Amount or value

50 drop-in passes plus a $225 credit for every eligible family member

How to apply

  1. Complete the online FAR form or download the paper application
  2. Attach proof of Coquitlam residence dated within two months
  3. Choose one accepted income-verification route and attach that evidence
  4. Submit online, by email, mail or in a sealed envelope at Glen Pine Pavilion
  5. After approval, use each family member's passes and credit in the recreation registration system

What you may need

  • Completed FAR application
  • Recent Coquitlam bill or government-issued identification
  • CRA Income Statement, assistance proof, red Compass Card proof, or adjudicator or agency section

Practical tips

  • You may redact irrelevant licence, SIN and bank-account numbers while leaving name, address, date and income visible
  • Use an Option C Income Statement, not a Notice of Assessment
  • Each eligible family member gets a separate set of passes and credit
