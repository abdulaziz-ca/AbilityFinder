Work-Able Accessible Employment Program (BC Public Service)

A paid internship program in the BC Public Service for recent post-secondary graduates who self-identify as having a disability — any physical, sensory, neurological, visible or invisible disability.

What it is

Work-Able coordinates paid internship opportunities across the BC Public Service for recent graduates with disabilities, for up to 24 months. Participants get government work experience, a peer cohort of other graduates with disabilities, exposure to different career paths, and post-program access to internal job postings.

Who it is for

Intake runs annually. Applications for the 2026/27 cohort are closed — the next intake for the 2027/28 cohort opens in spring 2027. Graduates keep access to internal BC Public Service job postings for 24 months after completing the program.

Amount or value

Paid BC Public Service internship of up to 24 months

How to apply

  1. Watch the Work-Able page for the annual intake (next opens spring 2027 for the 2027/28 cohort).
  2. Confirm you graduated within 3 years of the program start date.
  3. Apply online during the intake window; you self-identify as having a disability without disclosing a diagnosis.
  4. If selected, you are placed in a paid position with a ministry.

What you may need

  • Proof of post-secondary graduation (within 3 years of program start)
  • Resume

Practical tips

  • Contact the Work-Able team to be notified when the next application period opens.
  • Workplace accommodations are part of the program's design — you do not need to negotiate them alone.

Timing

Annual intake; internships run up to 24 months
